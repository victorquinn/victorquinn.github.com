Joint Infringement

Sep 28th, 2011

Over the course of this past summer, I completed an independent study in the field of patent litigation, specifically writing about the concept of Joint Infringement, a new and emerging area in patent litigation. For this study, I completed a lengthy research paper. As I have no intentions of having the paper formally published, I am attaching it here as it may be of use to practitioners in this field or to anyone else curious about this emerging area of law.

I am proud of the finished product and glad to have had the experience to study and learn about this new theory. I am also incredibly gracious to my sponsors for their assistance this paper though they shall remain nameless as I do not have their consent to mention them publicly and will respect their privacy.

One of the things that excites me about this area of law particularly is how fast things are changing and I get quite a rush from staying on top of it. Much like the fast-paced world of computer programming, it seems that things are changing in the realm of patent law with this, the America Invents Act, and with its attempt to keep up with the furious pace of technology. I enjoy being on this cutting edge and consider my enthusiasm for new developing areas to be one of my most valuable assets.

About Me

I grew up in Rochester, NY. My early career aspirations included nearly anything imaginable at one point or another, but I always gravitated toward the realms of science and logic.

Upon graduating from high school, I ventured to Massachusetts where I spent the next 10 years of my life becoming educated in Computer Science, Physics, and the Law.

In parallel, I spent most of those years working as a professional software engineer in the field of education, first at UMass Amherst and then at Amherst College.

After graduating from Law School, I moved down to Northern Virginia in search of warmer weather and a chance to enjoy life in a different region of the country. I also used this opportunity to change pace in my day-to-day, now working for NGP VAN, a company specializing in political software. I am truly enjoying bringing my experience developing for the education sector while at the same time learning about the entirely different realm of politics.

On the side I am working on building my new legal learning startup, Scales. I am also constantly expanding my knowledge of programming and the law while living with both my wife Susan and our great dane Stella.

Life is excellent and if you think otherwise, you're doing it wrong.
